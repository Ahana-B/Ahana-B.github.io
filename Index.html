AHANA BHATTACHARYA

An Aspiring Student

I'm Ahana

I am the product of a middle-class household, one among 350 million in India. However, something that was particularly different about my family was that we lived in many states of the country. In these distinct areas of the nation, I not only found diversity in culture and language, but also inequality, political distress and injustice at every corner. These festering social problems were increasingly ignored in an environment of immense poverty and illiteracy. This led to the development of an innocent drive at an early age. I felt the urge to explore the reasons behind such a vast difference in the level of comfort and quality of life from an underdeveloped state like Orissa to a metropolitan city like Mumbai.
